# The Kitchen — How to Use the App

*Your pickleball league, ranked. A simple guide to getting started.*

---

## What is The Kitchen?

The Kitchen is an app for running pickleball leagues. It tracks every match, automatically calculates fair player rankings (ELO + a DUPR-style 2.00–8.00 rating), runs tournaments with proper divisions, and keeps a live leaderboard your players can follow on their phones.

Think of it as the scorekeeper, ranking system, and tournament desk for your club — all in one place.

---

## 1. Creating your account

1. Open the app and tap **Create one free**.
2. Fill in your name, date of birth, gender (optional — needed for Men's/Women's/Mixed tournament divisions), email, and a password.
3. You're in. Your account works across every league you join.

> **Tip:** Your *nickname* is what shows on the leaderboard. Leave it blank to use your real name.

---

## 2. Leagues

A **league** is your club or group of players. Everything — rankings, matches, tournaments — happens inside a league.

### Creating a league (admins)
1. From your dashboard, tap **New league**.
2. Give it a name, location, and a banner colour.
3. You're now the **Head Admin**, with full control.

### Joining a league (players)
1. Ask your league admin for the **6-character invite code**.
2. On your dashboard, tap **Join league** and enter the code.
3. Your request goes to the admins for approval — once approved, you're on the board.

---

## 3. Roles

| Role | Can do |
|------|--------|
| **Head Admin** | Everything, plus promote/demote other admins. Usually the league creator. |
| **Admin** | Manage members, create matches & tournaments, edit league settings, approve join requests, ban players. |
| **Officiator** | Enter match scores and run assigned matches. |
| **Player** | Play, challenge others, view the leaderboard and their stats. |

Admins set each member's role from the **Members** tab.

---

## 4. Recording matches

1. An admin or officiator taps **New match** on the **Matches** tab.
2. Pick the format — **Singles, Doubles, or Mixed Doubles**.
3. Search and select the players for each team.
4. (Optional) Set a scheduled date/time, a court, and an officiator.
5. After the game, tap the match and **Submit score**.

The moment a score is saved, everyone's rating updates automatically.

> **Scores follow real pickleball rules:** games to 11, win by 2. The app won't accept an impossible score like 12–11.

> **Scheduling safeguard:** a player already booked in an untimed match can't be double-booked, and scheduled matches must be at least 30 minutes apart.

---

## 5. How rankings work

Every player starts at **1,000 points**. After each match, points move from the loser to the winner — and **how many** depends on how surprising the result was:

- Beat a stronger player → gain a lot.
- Beat a weaker player → gain a little.
- Win by a bigger margin → earn a bit more.

Your points map to a familiar **DUPR-style rating from 2.00 to 8.00** (1,000 points = 3.50). Singles and doubles are tracked separately, and the app remembers your **career-high** rating.

> Full details are on the **"How ELO Works"** page in the app menu — written in plain English, no maths degree required.

---

## 6. Challenges

Want a match against a specific player?

1. Go to the **Members** tab and tap **Challenge** next to their name.
2. Pick the format, choose an officiator, and optionally propose a time.
3. The officiator confirms first, then your opponent is notified to accept.
4. Once accepted, the match is automatically scheduled.

Track all your challenges — sent, received, and pending — on the **Challenges** tab.

---

## 7. Tournaments

Run a proper club tournament with multiple divisions.

### Creating a tournament (admins)
1. On the **Tournaments** tab, tap **New tournament**.
2. Name your event and pick divisions from the presets — **Open Men, Open Women, Open Mixed, 35+ Men, 35+ Mixed, Beginner Men/Women/Mixed, Novice Women, Junior 12 & Under** — or build your own.
3. Choose each division's format: **single elimination** (knockout) or **round robin** (everyone plays everyone — great for small groups).

### Registering (players)
- Players register themselves into any division they're **eligible** for. Eligibility (gender, age, skill level) is checked automatically.
- Doubles and Mixed divisions are entered as a **team** — pick your partner when registering.

### Running it (admins)
1. When registration closes, tap **Close registration & start** on each division.
2. The bracket generates automatically, **seeded by player rating** (top seeds may get a first-round bye).
3. Admins/officiators enter scores as games finish; winners advance automatically.

> **Why is a player already in Round 2?** They earned a **bye** — top seeds skip the first round when the bracket isn't perfectly full. They haven't won yet. (Full explanation on the in-app **"How Tournaments Work"** page.)

### Spectators
Every tournament has a **"Share with spectators"** link. Anyone with the link can follow the live bracket on their phone — no account or login needed.

---

## 8. Following along

- **Leaderboard** — live rankings, filterable by format, with recent-form dots.
- **Player profiles** — tap any player to see their rating history, win/loss record, and head-to-head results.
- **Notifications** — the bell keeps you posted on match schedules, challenges, and tournament entries.
- **Seasons** — admins can run seasons and the app saves a final-standings snapshot for each.
